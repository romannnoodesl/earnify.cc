Coinhive Alternatives: Best Browser Mining Solutions for 2026

Coinhive may be gone, but browser-based compute monetization is bigger than ever. We compare the top alternatives in 2026 — from open-source platforms to proprietary solutions — so you can find the right fit for your website.

Why Coinhive Still Matters

When Coinhive launched in September 2017, it introduced the world to browser-based cryptocurrency mining. Within months, it was deployed across tens of thousands of websites and generating millions in monthly revenue for publishers. Its shutdown in March 2019 left a massive void — and a lingering question: could browser mining ever be trusted again?

Six years later, the answer is a definitive yes. But the landscape has changed dramatically. Modern Coinhive alternatives are open source, privacy-first, and economically sustainable. They learned from Coinhive's mistakes: the 30% fee, the closed-source code, and the lack of transparency that enabled cryptojacking abuse.

Today's browser mining platforms are fundamentally different products. Here's how the top contenders compare.

What to Look for in a Coinhive Alternative

Before diving into specific platforms, it's worth understanding the criteria that separate a legitimate, modern solution from a Coinhive-era relic:

  • Open Source: Can you audit the code yourself? Closed-source miners are black boxes — you have no way to verify what they're doing in your users' browsers.
  • Fee Structure: Coinhive took 30%. Any platform charging more than 5% is repeating the same extractive model. The best platforms operate on single-digit fees.
  • Privacy Architecture: Does the platform collect personal data? Cookies? Fingerprinting? Privacy-compliant platforms collect zero personal information.
  • Pool Connectivity: Can you connect to your own mining pool, or are you locked into the platform's pool? Direct pool access means you control your payouts.
  • Algorithm Support: Modern ASIC-resistant algorithms (MinotaurX, Power2B, GhostRider) determine profitability more than any other factor.

2. CryptoLoot — The Proprietary Alternative

CryptoLoot

CryptoLoot emerged shortly after Coinhive's shutdown and has been a go-to for publishers seeking a drop-in replacement. It offers a similar JavaScript-based mining script with support for multiple algorithms. However, CryptoLoot remains fully proprietary — there is no public audit trail for its code. Its 10% fee is significantly higher than modern alternatives, and it uses tracking mechanisms that require GDPR consent.

Pros
  • Established track record
  • Easy to deploy
  • Multi-algorithm support
Cons
  • 10% fee — ten times Earnify
  • Closed source — no audit
  • Uses tracking / cookies

3. Coinimp — The Legacy Option

Coinimp

Coinimp has been operating since the Coinhive era and offers both a JavaScript miner and a dedicated mining software download. Its browser miner supports Monero (XMR) via CryptoNight-based algorithms, but it lacks support for modern ASIC-resistant algorithms like MinotaurX. With an 8% fee and proprietary codebase, Coinimp is a serviceable but aging option that hasn't kept pace with the industry's evolution toward transparency and privacy.

Pros
  • Long operational history
  • Desktop miner available
Cons
  • 8% fee
  • No modern algorithm support
  • Closed source
