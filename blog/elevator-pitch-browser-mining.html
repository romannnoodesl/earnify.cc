The Monetization Upsell Your Agency Is Missing

You build the sites. You maintain the sites. You should be earning from the sites — and Earnify makes it a 5-minute add-on to every project you ship.

The Pitch

"Your visitors' computers do background work for fractions of a cent — and you get paid for every second they stay on your site."

That's Earnify in one sentence. Every visitor who lands on a site you built runs lightweight cryptographic calculations in a background Web Worker. The site owner earns cryptocurrency based on total compute contributed. No banner ads. No pop-ups. No cookie consent dialogs. No tracking. Just a silent, passive revenue stream that runs on every page, for every visitor.

If that sentence is too technical for a client conversation, use the simpler version: "It's like a tip jar that fills itself while people read your content." Either way, the value proposition is immediate and concrete — turn existing traffic into money without changing a single thing about the site.

Why Earnify, Not Ads

When you propose monetization to a client, they'll default to "just add ads." Here's why Earnify is the better first option — and why it makes you look like the expert:

  • User experience: No layout shifts. No auto-playing videos. No intrusive banners. Earnify runs in a background thread invisible to the visitor. The site you carefully designed stays exactly as designed. Clients love this because it means their brand isn't cheapened by aggressive ad placements.
  • Privacy by design: Zero cookies. Zero tracking pixels. Zero personal data collected. No consent management platform, no GDPR banner, no CCPA disclosures. For clients who've been burned by compliance headaches — or who run European traffic — this is a deal-maker. Full legal breakdown in our GDPR compliance guide.
  • Ad-blocker proof: Over 42% of internet users now block ads. Those visitors generate exactly zero ad revenue. Earnify monetizes 100% of traffic — including the ones running uBlock Origin. On a site with 100K monthly sessions, that's recovering revenue from 42,000 visitors who were previously invisible to advertisers.

The line that lands: "Ads monetize attention. Earnify monetizes presence." Your clients don't have to choose one — they can run both. But for visitors who block ads, Earnify is the only channel that still works.

Risk Management: Built-In Answers to Hard Questions

Your clients will push back. Here are the answers before they ask:

  • "Will it slow down my site?" — No. Earnify runs on n−1 CPU threads, leaving the primary core free for UI rendering. The performance impact is negligible on modern hardware. Offer a Lighthouse before-and-after if they want proof.
  • "What about laptop battery?" — Earnify uses the Battery Status API to pause or throttle mining when a device is unplugged and discharging below a set threshold. It's a built-in safeguard, not a workaround. Clients see you've thought about their users, not just their revenue.
  • "Can visitors opt out?" — Yes. A visible toggle or a ?nomine=1 URL parameter lets anyone disable it instantly. This eliminates the biggest ethical concern upfront and strengthens your compliance posture — see the GDPR compliance guide for why opt-out mechanisms matter even when they aren't legally required.
  • "Is it legal?" — Yes. No personal data is collected, stored, or transmitted. The only network traffic is cryptographic work units submitted to a mining pool — mathematical artifacts with zero relationship to personal identity. Zero data means zero regulatory obligations under GDPR, CCPA, and ePrivacy.

Realistic Revenue Expectations

Honesty is what keeps clients. Don't oversell Earnify — sell it for what it is:

  • Per-visitor earnings are small — fractions of a cent per session. This is a volume play. A site with 10K monthly visitors will see modest returns. A site with 500K visitors will see meaningful baseline revenue.
  • Supplemental, not replacement — Earnify works best as a revenue floor, not a primary income source. Layer it on top of existing ad or subscription revenue to fill the gap that ad blockers create.
  • Longer sessions earn more — revenue scales with dwell time. Content sites, documentation portals, and SaaS dashboards outperform quick-bounce landing pages. This is by design: it incentivizes quality content.
  • Crypto volatility is real — earnings are denominated in cryptocurrency. Be upfront about this. Some clients will convert to fiat immediately; others will hold.

The honest framing: "This won't replace your ad revenue. It recovers the revenue you're already losing to ad blockers and adds a privacy-compliant baseline that never sleeps."
